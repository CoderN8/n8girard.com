Hydrogen Case Study

Company: Hypothetic

Duration: Sep 2022 - May 2024 (1yr 9mos)

Role: Director of Product

Challenge:

Hypothetic needed a tool for managing hundreds of thousands of 3D assets, finding specific assets efficiently, automatically grouping and organizing those assets, and reviewing the quality of those assets with their team so that they could build training data for 3D ML algorithms.

Objectives:

  • Hypothetic desired to run training on data it had acquired after it organized the data into suitable collections designed for specific training purposes.
  • Hypothetic desired to leverage this tool for managing assets for a large third party contract involving 20K assets being generated.
  • Hypothetic desired to leverage this tool as a basis for a web-based asset management and reviewing platform.

Approach:

  • Hypothetic conducted competitive analysis against multiple 3D Digital Asset Managment Systems (DAMs) on the market, and dissected ML research papers looking for innovative solutions to our problem areas.
  • Team planned to apply innovative ML search algorithms to solve the search problem, automated labeling, automated organization, and use an AI agent connected to a powerful API and chat interface to put the power in the hands of the user.
  • Team planned to leverage multiple Web3 technologies like React, BablylonJS, ThreeJS, and web friendly formats like GLB and GLTF to visualize 3D assets on a web interface and allow teams to comment on their quality.

Solution:

  • UX team went through several iterations on the design using Figma focusing on simplicity and clarity and then built prototypes and vetted them with industry professional prospects and customers as well prior to committing to a final design on any one feature as the design evolved along the way throughout the build process.
  • Dev team started with Bootstrap but quickly started building a custom component library to replace it due to design demands.
  • Dev team also started with BabylonJS but shifted to ThreeJS due to better capabilities, maintenance, and support for more formats.
  • Dev team built the entire infrastructure on top of AWS docker containers for easy scaling and deployment.

Results:

  • Hypothetic was able to curate collections of several thousand 3D assets in days and trained several ML algorithms from them. They were also able to auto-organize assets, automatically detect duplicates, auto-rotate them, auto-tag them, and search for similar in addition to regular search with less than 300 ms latency.
  • Hypothetic's client managed their project with over 20K+ assets, curating them at a 5x efficiently gain in a team that spanned the globe, and saved 6+ months on their project timeline.
  • Hypothetic deployed the tool for a limited beta release to a select few customers to get feedback prior to deploying to a wider audience their 3D DAM solution.

Factory Case Study

Company: Hypothetic

Duration: Oct 2022 - Apr 2024 (1yr 7mos)

Role: Director of Product

Challenge:

Hypothetic needed a tool for procedurally generating thousands of 3D assets based on dynamically assembling interchangable parts to accommodate a rule set and rarity for creating a 3D NFT collection.

Objectives:

  • Hypothetic desired to generate thousands of 3D assets with a high degree of artistic control for professionals for their own clients.
  • Hypothetic desired to leverage this tool to offset the workload of one of their clients who was working on an extensive NFT collectables project.
  • Hypothetic desired to speed up the production pipeline by automating the generation process while giving the artists direct control over the details of their collections.

Approach:

  • Hypothetic conducted competitive analysis against multiple 2D NFT generators on the market looking for innovative solutions to our problem areas.
  • Team planned to apply innovative procedural generation algorithms that accommodated a strict rule set and guided additionally by rarity settings to provide complete control over the number of assets generated and the quality and rarity of the parts in those assets.
  • Team planned to leverage multiple Web3 technologies like React, ThreeJS, and web friendly formats like GLB to visualize 3D assets on a web interface and export them to their other product, Hydrogen.

Solution:

  • UX team went through several iterations on the design using Figma focusing on simplicity and clarity and then built prototypes and vetted them with industry professional prospects and customers as well prior to committing to a final design on any one feature as the design evolved along the way throughout the build process.
  • Dev team started building a custom component library due to highly-detailed design demands.
  • Dev team also started with React, Rust, and ThreeJS due to performance capabilities, maintenance, and quality control.
  • Dev team built the entire infrastructure on top of AWS docker containers for easy scaling and deployment.

Results:

  • Hypothetic's client was able to generate collections of several thousand 3D assets in days and exported them to Hydrogen for curation.
  • Hypothetic's client managed their project with over 20K+ assets, curating them at a 5x efficiently gain in a team that spanned the globe, and saved 6+ months on their project timeline.
  • Hypothetic deployed the tool for a limited beta release to a select few customers to get feedback prior to deploying to a wider audience.

Aufero Case Study

Company: Aufero

Duration: Mar 2020 - May 2022 (2yr 3mos)

Role: Chief Experience Officer

Challenge:

Aufero needed to find a solution for lowering the risk on monolithic codebase deployments for Fintech, Healthcare, and other high-risk deployment environments.

Objectives:

  • Aufero desired to build a deployment solution that was controlled by Product Managers rather than IT or DevOps.
  • Aufero desired to lower the risk of deployments by deploying to the same shared environment rather than different staging and production environments.
  • Aufero desired to build a solution that would circumvent IT roadblocks and integrate automatically with the CI/CD build process.

Approach:

  • Aufero conducted multiple user test interviews to verify the pain point we were addressing.
  • Team planned to leverage Rust to build a zero latency traffic router which would direct traffic between deployment instances on the same environment.
  • Team planned to build a web interface that was Product Manager-friendly that integrated into the CI/CD process to allow for management of releases.

Solution:

  • UX team went through several iterations on the design using Figma focusing on simplicity and clarity and then built prototypes for communicating design requirements to the dev team.
  • Dev team built the infrastructure and router to direct traffic based on audience metrics being passed in on HTML traffic headers.
  • Dev team built the web interface using React and Material UI component library that would be easy to navigate with self onboarding and analytics through Pendo.
  • Dev team built the entire infrastructure on top of AWS docker containers for easy scaling and deployment.

Results:

  • Aufero was able to build a zero latency traffic routing solution that enabled deployments to coexist on the same production environment.
  • Aufero was able to build a Product Manager-friendly web interface for managing deployments integrated in the CI/CD pipeline that significantly lowered the risk of deployment issues for monolithic codebases while maintaining service level agreements with existing risk-adverse customers.
  • Aufero deployed the tool for public release.
